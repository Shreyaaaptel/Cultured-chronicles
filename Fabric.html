types of textiles

Weaving

Weaving refers to the process of interlacing yarn to produce textiles and fabrics. The vertical thread is called the warp, while the horizontal thread is referred to as the weft. Warp and weft are usually laced in fixed patterns using a loom.A simple criss-crossing of the warp and the weft at right angles is called plain-weave.
Example : Patola Weave

Embroidery

An ancient Indian art involving the process of decorating fabric with materials like threads, pearls, beads, quills and sequins. The fabrics and decorating material used in traditional embroidery vary from region to region.
Exmaple : Zardozi, Aari Embroidery

Painting

This is the art of hand painting textiles and fabrics usually using natural colors. Traditionally, painted textiles have been used as large temple hangings with depictions from mythology, folklore and religious scriptures. They are also used as wall hangings for decoration purposes, bed covers and even garments.
Example : Kalamkari, Madhubani Painting

Dyeing

Dyeing is a process of colouring a textile or a fabric material by applying dye on its surface. Generally, dyeing is done by dissolving a dye in water and dipping the fabric in this solution many times to fix the color. Dyes differ in their resistance to sunlight, washing, gas, alkalies and other agents. Dyes are mainly of two types- natural and synthetic.
Example : Lehariya, Gharchola

Printing

The term printing is used to signify production by using various coloured patterns or designs upon all sorts of fabrics such as cotton, linen, silk, jute and various combinations of these with other fibres. Each print has a unique technique and design resulting in a distinctive style.
Example : Ajrakh print, Bagh print
